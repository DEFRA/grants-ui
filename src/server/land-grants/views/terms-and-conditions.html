These agreement terms and conditions set out the rules that you and the Rural Payments Agency (RPA) must follow for the duration of your agreement. They apply to agreements for the Farm Payments Technical Test.

1. Introduction

1.1 The Farm Payments Technical Test

The Farm Payments Technical Test is a private beta run by the Rural Payments Agency (referred to below as “us”, “we”, “our”) on behalf of the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (“Defra”). It is part of the Environmental Land Management Scheme under section 1 of the Agriculture Act 2020 and in conjunction with the Agriculture (Financial Assistance) Regulations 2021 (“the Regulations”).

1.2 About these Terms and Conditions

  1. The agreement (the “Agreement”) comprises:
    1. the “Agreement Document”, which describes the land parcels in the Agreement (the “Agreement Land”) and the sum to be paid to you (the “Grant”) for delivering the action(s) you have selected (the “Actions”);
    2. these “Terms and Conditions”, which are applicable to the payment of the Grant; and
    3. the Actions
  2. If there is any conflict between these Terms and Conditions, the Agreement Document and the Actions, these Terms and Conditions will be given priority.
  3. When the agreement holder (“you”) enters the Agreement, you are agreeing to the terms applicable to the payment of the Grant.

1.3 Why you should read the Terms and Conditions

You should read the Terms and Conditions carefully because they provide information including:

  1. how we will pay the Grant to you, as set out in your Agreement Document;
  2. how you or we may change or end the Agreement; and
  3. what you need to do if you cannot comply with your obligations under the Agreement.

1.4 References in the Terms and Conditions

  1. Definitions of capitalised terms are defined in this document when they are first referred to and can also be found in the definition of terms at the end of these Terms and Conditions.
  2. A reference to the “Law” means any law, statute, subordinate legislation within the meaning of Section 21(1) of the Interpretation Act 1978, byelaw, right within the meaning of Section 4(1) EU Withdrawal Act 2018 as amended by EU (Withdrawal Agreement) Act 2020, regulation, order, regulatory policy, statutory guidance which has the effect of law, judgement of a relevant court of law, or directives or requirements of any regulatory body with which you are required to comply.
  3. A reference to a statute or statutory provision is a reference to it as amended, extended or re-enacted from time to time and shall include all subordinate legislation made under that statute or statutory provision.
  4. A reference to a public organisation includes a reference to any successor to that public organisation.
  5. Any words following the terms ‘including’, ‘include’, ‘in particular’ or ‘for example’ or any similar phrase shall be construed as illustrative and shall not limit the generality of the related general words.
  6. Unless the context otherwise requires, words in the singular shall include the plural and in the plural shall include the singular.

  1. We have provided information relating to the Farm Payments Technical Test to you (the "Farm Payments Technical Test Information"). The Farm Payments Technical Test Information includes:
    1. the Actions;
    2. the Farm Payments Rules that further explain the mandatory requirements of your Agreement as established by these Terms and Conditions. The Farm Payment Rules are contained in the following sections:
      • section 1 ‘About the Farm payments technical test’;
      • section 2 ‘When and how much you’ll get paid’;

However, the version applicable to you shall be the version which was current when the Agreement commences (the “Agreement Start Date”), unless amended in accordance with condition 5 (Changes to your Agreement).

2. About your Agreement

2.1 Your Agreement

The Agreement comprises:

  1. the Agreement Document, including any documents referenced in the Agreement Document;
  2. these Terms and Conditions (which are further explained in the Farm Payments Rules as explained in condition 1.5(b)); and
  3. the Actions you have selected.

2.2 What you are declaring

By entering the Agreement you confirm that:

  1. the declarations you made in your Farm Payments Technical Test application for the Grant remain true and accurate to the best of your knowledge and belief;
  2. you have full capacity and authority to enter into the Agreement;
  3. you are not aware of any circumstances which would render you ineligible for the Grant or otherwise prevent you from complying with your obligations under the Agreement;
  4. your obligations under the Agreement do not and will not conflict in whole or in part with any other legal or contractual obligations you are subject to; and
  5. you will at all times comply with all relevant Law in the performance of your obligations under the Agreement.

2.3 What you are agreeing to do

In applying for and receiving the Grant, you are agreeing to:

  1. use the Grant to complete your selected Actions (and for associated administrative costs), as referenced in your Agreement Document and supplemented by the Farm Payments Information;
  2. comply with these Terms and Conditions; and
  3. obtain, maintain and comply with any permits, licences, permissions, consents, approvals, certificates and authorisations (whether statutory or otherwise) ("Permissions") which are required:
    1. to comply with the obligations under the Agreement; and
    2. for the protection of ancient monuments, archaeological sites, heritage sites, trees, historical sites on the Agreement Land and Sites of Special Scientific Interest (SSSIs). In relation to SSSIs and scheduled monuments, you are not required to have any required Permissions prior to the Agreement Start Date but you must not undertake any Actions until it has been received from the relevant authority.

2.4 Agreement Period

  1. The Agreement is for a period of 12 months from the Agreement Start Date unless terminated earlier under condition 13 (Termination of an Agreement) (the “Agreement Period”). The Agreement Start Date is shown in your Agreement Document.

2.5 This Agreement does not replace the need for regulatory consents

You understand that the Agreement does not operate as:

  1. an SSSI consent. If such consent is required you must apply separately in accordance with any instructions provided by Natural England;
  2. a scheduled monument consent of the Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Areas Act 1979 (or other consent under that Act). If such consent is required you must apply separately in accordance with any instructions provided by Historic England. You will obtain and take account of the SFI HEFER consultation response for all the land and features in connection to this Agreement; or
  3. any other regulatory consent required by the Law.

3. Management Control of the Agreement Land

3.1 What is Management Control?

You will have “Management Control” if you have sufficient control over how the Agreement Land is managed to complete the Actions you have selected. This is explained in the section ‘Check if you have management control of land’ in the Farm Payment Rules.

3.2 Management Control for the duration of the Agreement

You must have Management Control of the Agreement Land for the entire Agreement Period.

3.3 If you lose Management Control

  1. If you lose Management Control of all or part of the Agreement Land before the Agreement End Date, you must inform us using the procedure in condition 6 (Change of Circumstances).
  2. If you lose Management Control of all of the Agreement Land, the Agreement will be terminated, and you may have to repay some or all of the Grant already received as set out in condition 6 (Change of Circumstances).
  3. If you lose Management Control of part of the Agreement Land, the affected land will be removed from the Agreement and you may have to repay some or all of the Grant already received as set out in condition 6 (Change of Circumstances).

3.4 Tenanted Land

If you hold land under a Farm Business Tenancy under the Agricultural Tenancies Act 1995 or an Agricultural Holdings Act 1986 tenancy:

  1. you must ensure that by entering the land into the Agreement you do not breach the conditions of your tenancy; and
  2. if the tenancy expires before the Agreement End Date, you must only include this land in your Agreement Land if you expect to have Management Control of this land for the entire Agreement Period.

4. Annual Declaration

4.1 What is the Annual Declaration?

Your “Annual Declaration” confirms that you have complied, or expect to have complied, with your obligations under the Agreement during the Agreement Period. This is explained in the section about ‘Managing your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.

4.2 When to submit an Annual Declaration

You must submit your Annual Declaration to us once during the Agreement Period. You must do this by a date that we will specify (the “Due Date”) which will be within the last two (2) months of the Agreement Period.

4.3 What happens if you do not submit your Annual Declaration

If you do not submit your Annual Declaration by the Due Date, it may affect the Grant paid to you as set out in condition 7 (Payments), and we may also treat this as a breach of your obligations under the Agreement under condition 10.

5. Changes to your Agreement

5.1 Removing Actions or land from the Agreement

  1. During the Agreement Period, we will not usually allow you to remove an Action or remove land from the Agreement Land. This is explained in the section about ‘Managing your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.
  2. If you want to remove an Action or remove land forming part of the Agreement Land, you must write to us to request this and explain (providing any supporting evidence) why you wish to do so.
  3. We will consider requests under condition 5.1 on a case-by-case basis and may allow the request if there is a Change of Circumstances as defined in condition 6. We will inform you of our decision within a reasonable time period.
  4. If we approve a request to remove an Action or remove land forming part of the Agreement Land, then we may reduce, recover or withhold payment of the Grant in whole or in part until that request has been processed and the Agreement amended accordingly.

5.2 When we may change your Agreement

  1. We will not usually seek to vary your Agreement during the Agreement Period (in a way not referred to elsewhere in these Terms and Conditions). This is explained in the section about ‘Managing your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.
  2. However, there may also be exceptional circumstances (not referred to elsewhere in these Terms and Conditions) where we have to vary your Agreement during the Agreement Period. We will only do this due to exceptional circumstances, such as:
    1. changes in Law which must be reflected in the Agreement; and/or
    2. the need to respond to wider government emergency measures and/or measures imperative to the national interest, for example, disease outbreaks or food security emergencies.
  3. If we vary the Agreement under condition 5.2(b):
    1. we will notify you in writing and will endeavour to give such notice as is reasonable and proportionate, having regard to the nature of the variation, its consequences for you and any action required from you;
    2. any variation to the Agreement will apply from the date we specify in our notice to you, provided you take the action required as set out in the notice; and
    3. if the variation to the Agreement under condition 5.2(b) is unacceptable to you, you can terminate your Agreement by notifying us in writing within a reasonable period that we will specify in our notice to you. If you do not accept the variation in the manner set out in our notification, we may terminate your Agreement in accordance with condition 13.1(b). Condition 13.3(d) will not apply, provided you inform us within the period specified by us.

5.3 When we may temporarily adjust your Agreement

  1. We may identify factors which may temporarily impact your ability to complete your selected Actions, meaning you are temporarily unable to comply with your obligations under the Agreement. These factors may include, but are not limited to, adverse weather conditions such as flooding or drought. This is explained in the section about ‘Managing your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.
  2. If condition 5.3(a) applies, we may make a temporary adjustment to the Actions in the Agreement, with this temporary adjustment only being effective during a time period specified by us.
  3. If we make a temporary adjustment under condition 5.3(b) we will notify you and will endeavour to give such notice as is reasonable and proportionate. Our notice will set out:
    1. the details of the temporary adjustment;
    2. the time period for when the temporary adjustment applies, including a start date and end date;
    3. the evidence you must keep if you choose to comply with the temporary adjustment, as described under condition 5.3(f).
  4. The temporary adjustment under condition 5.3(b) will cease to apply after the end date specified in our notice under condition 5.3(c). After that end date, you must complete your selected Actions to comply with your obligations under the Agreement as if no temporary adjustment had been made.
  5. There is no obligation for you to comply with the temporary adjustment under condition 5.3(b). You can continue to complete your selected Actions without complying with the temporary adjustment under Condition 5.3(b).
  6. If you choose to comply with the temporary adjustment under Condition 5.3(b), you must:
    1. keep Records as set out in condition 18 (What accounts and records you must keep) to evidence your compliance with condition 5.3; and
    2. complete and retain a copy of the prescribed version we publish under condition 5.3(c)(iii).

6. Change of Circumstances

6.1 What is a Change of Circumstances

A “Change of Circumstances” means something happens within or outside your control that might reasonably be expected to affect:

  1. your eligibility for the Grant;
  2. the Grant you should receive;
  3. your ability to complete the Actions; or
  4. your ability to comply with these Terms and Conditions.

This is explained in the section about ‘‘Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.

6.2 Notifying us of a Change of Circumstances

If condition 6.1 applies, you must notify us in writing as soon as reasonably practicable and in any event within eight (8) weeks of the Change of Circumstances occurring.

6.3 Action we may take when there is a Change of Circumstances

Where you have notified us about a Change of Circumstances, we may take one or more of the following actions:

  1. amend, remove or replace a condition subject to which the Grant is given either temporarily or permanently, including by varying the area of Agreement Land;
  2. amend, remove or replace the Actions or timescales either temporarily or permanently;
  3. reduce the Grant either temporarily or permanently;
  4. require you to repay some or all of the Grant already paid to you; and/or
  5. terminate the Agreement before the Agreement End Date.

6.4 When we will notify you of our decision

We will notify you within a reasonable period of our “Decision” to take any of the actions under condition 6.3 and the reasons for it. At the same time, we will remind you of your rights to request a reconsideration under condition 12 (Reconsideration of Decisions and Determinations).

7. Payments

7.1 Frequency of payments

The total Grant is divided into four (4) quarterly instalments. The first instalment of the Grant will usually be made in the fourth (4th) month after the Agreement Start Date if you have complied with the obligations under the Agreement and shall continue on this basis. This is explained under the section about ‘When and how much you’ll get paid’ in the Farm Payment Rules.

7.2 Where payments will be made

We will pay the Grant directly to your bank or building society account that is registered with us via BACS transfer, subject to the necessary funds being available when the payment falls due.

7.3 When payments may be delayed, reduced, recovered or withheld

We may delay, reduce, recover or withhold the payment of the Grant in whole or in part if you do not submit the Annual Declaration required under condition 4; and/or you breach your Agreement under condition 10.

7.4 Variation of payment rates

  1. As stated in the Farm Payments Information we do not expect to vary the Grant during the Agreement Period.
  2. However, we reserve the right, under exceptional circumstances not referred to elsewhere in these Terms and Conditions, to vary the Grant during the Agreement Period, in accordance with condition 5.2.

It is your responsibility to check all the payments of the Grant we make to you and to notify us within two (2) weeks of you becoming aware if you have any reason to believe an error has occurred. You must repay any overpayment or payment of the Grant to which you are not entitled (including due to our administrative error).

8.2 When a repayment must be made

Any sum that becomes repayable under condition 8.1 will be treated as a debt by you to us and if it is not repaid we may issue a recovery order in respect of it.

9. Checking you are complying with your Agreement

9.1 What we may check

We may check your compliance with the obligations under the Agreement, including that:

  1. you are complying with these Terms and Conditions; and/or
  2. you have completed what is required in each of your selected Actions in a way that could reasonably be expected to achieve their aims.

9.2 How we will check you are complying with your obligations under the Agreement

We will check you are complying with the obligations under the Agreement through a combination of physical and virtual site visits, remote monitoring, and desk-based administrative checks. To help us do this, you must:

  1. for virtual or physical site visits:
    1. allow a person authorised by us to carry out site visits and exercise powers of entry under the Regulations (‘Authorised Person’) access to any Agreement Land, premises, livestock, trees, crops, plants, machinery, equipment, documents or records (or any related apparatus) covered by the Agreement. The Authorised Person may be accompanied by such other persons as they consider necessary; and
    2. provide any assistance reasonably requested by an Authorised Person.

The section about ‘Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules explains how we will check delivery of your obligations under the Agreement.

We will endeavour to agree a suitable date and time for the site visit with you. Where it is not possible to agree a suitable date and time, we will notify you in writing of the purpose of the site visit and the date and time at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance of the site visit. Regulation 17 of the Regulations (opens in new tab) allows us to access your land at any reasonable hour without notice if, for example, we have a reasonable suspicion that you have committed a serious breach, fraud or any other related offence.

9.4 What we may do during a site visit

Regulation 18 of the Regulations (opens in new tab) explains what an Authorised Person can do during a site visit.

10. A breach of your Agreement

10.1 What happens if we suspect there may have been a breach of your Agreement

  1. Where we reasonably suspect you have breached the Agreement, we will carry out a proportionate investigation. We explain what happens if we suspect a breach of the Agreement in the section about ‘Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.
  2. If we reasonably suspect you have breached the Agreement, we may withhold the Grant until such investigation is concluded, however we will not usually do this.
  3. We will inform you of the outcome of the investigation carried out under condition 10.1(a) within a reasonable period.

10.2 How you could breach your Agreement

After we carry out a proportionate investigation, we may make a “Determination” that you have breached the Agreement if any of the following applies:

  1. you have not notified us of a Change of Circumstances under condition 6;
  2. you have, at any time, given us false or misleading information;
  3. you have used the Grant for a purpose that is not in accordance with the Agreement;
  4. you have used the Grant for activities that breach any Law with which you are required to comply;
  5. you have failed to comply with the terms of your Agreement;
  6. you have breached any requirement to which you are subject under the Agriculture (Financial Assistance) Regulations 2021;
  7. you have failed to provide information, records or evidence required under condition 18;
  8. you have prevented an Authorised Person from carrying out a virtual or physical site visit of the Agreement Land;
  9. you have refused to provide assistance reasonably requested by an Authorised Person carrying out a virtual or physical site visit of the Agreement Land;
  10. you have otherwise obstructed a virtual or physical site visit of the Agreement Land; or
  11. you have failed to prevent or report actual or anticipated fraud or corruption in relation to the Grant.

10.3 A breach caused by your employees or agents

The circumstances described in condition 10.2(b) to condition 10.2(j) apply, where appropriate, to any action taken by your employees or agents.

10.4 What happens if we determine there has been a breach

If we make a Determination that you have breached the Agreement, we will write to inform you of this within a reasonable period. We will give you the reasons for this Determination. We will then decide what action needs to be taken, if any. We explain what happens if we determine you have breached the Agreement in the section ‘13. Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules. You may respond to any Determination in accordance with condition 12.

10.5 Actions we may take if you breach your Agreement

  1. There are no set actions for particular breaches of the Agreement, and we will assess all the circumstances of a breach in a fair and consistent manner, on a case-by-case basis and take the action(s) we think is/are appropriate. This is explained in the section about ‘Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.
  2. If we make a Determination that you have breached the Agreement, we may take any of the following actions:
    1. allow you to rectify the breach;
    2. issue you with a warning letter;
    3. amend, remove or replace a condition subject to which the Grant is given either temporarily or permanently;
    4. amend, remove or replace the Actions or timescales either temporarily or permanently;
    5. delay, reduce, recover or withhold payment or require repayment of all or part of the Grant;
    6. terminate the Agreement before the Agreement End Date; and/or
    7. prohibit you from receiving financial assistance from other financial assistance schemes under the Agriculture Act 2020 for a period of up to two (2) years from the day after we notify you of the Determination on the breach.

10.6 Repayment if there has been a breach

If we require you to repay all or part of the Grant under condition 10.5(b)(v), then you must do so within sixty (60) days of the date of our demand for repayment. If you fail to repay the requested amount within sixty (60) days of our demand, then that sum will be recoverable as a civil debt with interest. Our right to recover under condition 10.5(b)(v) extends to the whole of the Grant paid to you.

11. Good reasons for a breach

11.1 What are good reasons for a breach

We understand that there may be circumstances (a “Good Reason”) where you are in breach of the obligations under the Agreement because of events outside of your control.

  1. A Good Reason for a breach of the Agreement includes, but is not limited to:
    1. natural events, such as prolonged adverse weather conditions, flooding, or animal or plant disease;
    2. if you are seriously ill;
    3. if you die;
    4. if there is unforeseen loss of Management Control of the Agreement Land, for example when a tenancy is terminated, or because of the compulsory purchase of land by a third party;
    5. financial circumstances, such as bankruptcy;
    6. evidenced supply chain problems, such as a lack of soil testing capacity; and
    7. criminal damage by a third party, such as arson or vandalism.
  2. We explain what good reasons for a breach could include in the section about ‘Complying with your FPTT agreement’ in the Farm Payments Rules.

11.2 Notifying us about a good reason for a breach

  1. If condition 11.1 applies, you (or any person authorised to act for you) must notify us in writing to explain the Good Reason.
  2. If the Good Reason relates to a breach of the Agreement under condition 10.2(h), condition 10.2(i) or condition 10.2(j), you must notify us in writing immediately.
  3. In all other cases, you must notify us in writing within eight (8) weeks from:
    1. the date you are able to; or
    2. the date we notify you of the Determination on the breach of the Agreement.

12. Reconsideration of Decisions and Determinations

12.1 How to ask us to reconsider a Decision or Determination

If you would like us to reconsider any Decision (for a Change of Circumstances under condition 6) or Determination (for a breach of the Agreement under condition 10) that we have made, you must use our complaints procedure (a copy of which has been provided to you) to ask for it to be reconsidered.

12.2 When to ask us to reconsider a Decision or Determination

You must ask us to reconsider any Decision or Determination within sixty (60) calendar days of the date you are notified about our Decision or Determination.

12.3 How we will reconsider a Decision or Determination

We will consider your request to reconsider our Decision or Determination carefully, including any information or evidence you have provided to us. We will confirm the outcome of our reconsideration, with reasons, in writing within a reasonable period.

12.4. How to appeal the outcome of a reconsideration

You may appeal the outcome of our reconsideration of our Decision or Determination within sixty (60) calendar days of the date you are notified of that outcome where you believe the outcome of our reconsideration:

  1. was based on an error of fact;
  2. was wrong in Law; or
  3. there has been a material procedural error.

12.5 When we will confirm the outcome of your appeal

We will confirm the outcome of your appeal under condition 12.4 within a reasonable period in writing explaining the reasons for this outcome, and this will be our final determination.

13. Termination of an Agreement

13.1 When we may terminate your Agreement

  1. We may terminate the Agreement before the Agreement End Date on written notice to you without compensation and with immediate effect if:

    2. there has been a Change of Circumstances under condition 6 meaning you are no longer eligible to receive the Grant;

    4. you have not repaid to us a sum that has become recoverable pursuant to condition 10 or condition 8.

13.2 What happens if we terminate your Agreement

  1. Where we terminate the Agreement under condition 13.1(a), you may be required to repay some or all of the Grant already paid to you.
  2. Where we terminate the Agreement under condition 13.1(b), you will be able to keep the Grant already paid to you and we will also pay to you the next scheduled quarterly instalment of the Grant which would have been payable by us but for the termination, provided that you are not in breach of the Agreement and have submitted the Annual Declaration.

13.3 What happens if you want to terminate your Agreement

  1. We will not usually allow you to terminate your Agreement before the Agreement End Date.
  2. If you want to terminate your Agreement before the Agreement End Date, you must write to us to request this and explain (providing supporting evidence) why you wish to do so.
  3. We will consider requests under condition 13.3 on a case-by-case basis and may allow the request if there is a Change of Circumstances (condition 6). We will inform you of our decision within a reasonable time period.
  4. If we agree to terminate your Agreement before the Agreement End Date, you may be required to repay all or part of the Grant, and you will remain bound by the relevant obligations under the Agreement until you make this repayment.
  5. Condition 13.3(d) does not apply to condition 5.2 except as set out in condition 5.2(c)(iii).

13.4 This Agreement will automatically terminate in the event of your death if you have entered this Agreement in your own name as an individual.

This includes our obligation to pay the final quarterly instalment of the Grant provided that you have submitted your Annual Declaration and have not breached your Agreement.
